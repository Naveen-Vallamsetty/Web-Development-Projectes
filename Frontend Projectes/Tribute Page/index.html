A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam is a famous name in the whole world. He is counted among the greatest scientists of the 21st century. Even more, he becomes the 11th president of India and served his country. He was the most valued person of the country as his contribution as a scientist and as a president is beyond compare. Apart from that, his contribution to the ISRO (Indian Space Research Organization) is remarkable. He headed many projects that contributed to the society also he was the one who helped in the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles. For his involvement in the Nuclear power in India, he was known as “Missile Man of India”. And due to his contribution to the country, the government awarded him with the highest civilian award.

Biography

  • Dr. Abdul Kalam was a former President of India.
  • He was a well-known aerospace scientist.
  • He was born on October 15, 1931 in a town called Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu.
  • His full name was Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam.
  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • He was a beloved leader and was known as a people’s president.
  • Dr. Kalam played a very important role in India’s defense research and India’s nuclear tests in 1998.
  • He was popularly called the ‘Missile Man of India’ for his contributions to developing Indigenously built cruise missiles such as Agni and Prithvi.
  • He was awarded India’s highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contributions to the country.
  • He died on July 27, 2015 at the age of 83, from a heart attack.
